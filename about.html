About

Ever since reading through a boxed set of hardcover science books in my childhood, I have been fascinated with science and technology. The beauty of the universe and planet we inhabit, the incredible technologies that have enabled modern society, and even mathematics are all subjects to which I fully ascribe the mantra of "life-long learning."

However, it wasn't until my high school years that I finally decided on my career path. After exposure to the philosophy of existentialism, and internally reaching the conclusion that the advancement and application of science was one of the best ways to contribute to society, I resolved to become an engineer. I later graduated from Caltech with a BS in mechanical engineering in 2018, and then UCLA with an MS in aerospace engineering in 2019. Currently, I am looking for my first full-time position, where I hope to apply my skills at innovative organizations and help do my part in bettering the world through engineering.

Core Skills

CAD

Engineers of all fields use computer-aided design software to draft and design various products. For my projects, I primarily used SolidWorks CAD to create 3D models and 2D engineering drawings of prototypes to manufacture in the machine shop.

FEA

Finite element analysis is the predominant simulation technique in engineering. I have taken courses in the theory and application of FEA, and have exposure in the use of ANSYS and COMSOL in both solid and fluid mechanics.

Machining

Machining is a manufacturing method usually used to create parts out of metal stock material. I used my university machine shop's mills, lathes, waterjet cutters, 3D printers, and other machines extensively for project courses.

Programming

My programming experience started in high school, where classes focused on object-oriented programming. In college, this expanded to include data analysis and simulation. Languages I've used include Java, MatLab, Python, C++, and others.
