Work Highlights

Robstacle Course

ME72 is the capstone design project for Caltech mechanical engineering undergrads, and has rightfully earned an infamous reputation as a very high workload course. In my year, teams were tasked with the design, manufacture, operation, and everything in between of three robots. These robots worked in tandem to complete an obstacle course, and at the same time ferried a baton to a goal, akin to a relay race. On competition day, my team was one of only two able to complete the obstacle course in its entirety, out of five teams.

AAReST Cubesat

For my undergrad aerospace engineering minor, I participated in a spacecraft design course. Part of the course involved assisting with the work being done on Caltech's AAReST cubesat (Autonomous Assembly of a Reconfigurable Space Telescope), which is intended to demonstrate the concept of in-orbit assembly of large spacecraft. I was assigned to the telecom team, and worked to optimize AAReST's antenna design through simulation and network analyzer experimentation with an EM model of the cubesat that I built.

Mechanical Properties of Hiperco

Hiperco is an iron-cobalt alloy with exceptional magnetic properties, but its mechanical behavior leaves much to be desired and is still poorly understood. During my summer internship at Sandia National Laboratories, I worked with the Materials, Mechanics, and Tribology Department to study Hiperco's mechanical properties through a novel experimental technique. I worked with staff and technologists to prepare samples, set up experiments with a novel technique, and process and analyze data.
