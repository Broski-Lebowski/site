Bench Power Supply

Personal Project to make a Bench Power Supply

This project is still in progress.

I recently realized how useful a bench power supply could be, for everything from charging all kinds of batteries to performing electrolysis. Furthermore, I felt my knowledge in electrical engineering was lacking, so I thought this was a perfect opportunity to both make something useful and learn at the same time.

There are a plethora of resources online for DIY power supplies; most of them are modified ATX power supplies. ATX is desktop computer standard developed by Intel in 1995, and specifies various parameters for the motherboard, power supply unit, and their connectors. I had an old, hardly-used desktop computer in the garage that I could take apart for this purpose. When I opened up the case however, I was amazed to find that the computer was not ATX; rather, it was built off the even older AT standard. Luckily, the AT PSU's output current of 24A at +5V and 10A at +12V was still quite sufficient.

The documentation for AT power supplies is a lot sparser compared to that of ATX, but the differences are not too extreme. Most of the relevant components, such as the pinout/output, are very similar, with minor differences that are unimportant for this purpose. The main difference is the power on method, for which the AT desktop computer uses a power button, without a power on switch.
